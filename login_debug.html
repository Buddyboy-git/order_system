More Boar’s Head listeria contamination problems

Thumann Distributors,

Boar’s Head is once again facing significant issues related to listeria contamination, even after last year’s outbreak that tragically resulted in 10 deaths. Most recently, the Ambriola Company issued a recall for several of its Pecorino Romano cheese products which they supply to Boar’s Head. In response, Boar’s Head has initiated a recall of its Grated Pecorino Romano Cheese and FS Grated Romano Cheese.

As these ongoing problems continue to arise, Boar’s Head has reportedly paid out millions of dollars in hush-hush payouts to families of victims who lost loved ones due to the tainted deli meat. This includes a $4 million payout to a Long Island widow after her 73-year-old husband passed away just six days after eating a Boar’s Head sandwich.

We have provided a link to the full article below. We strongly encourage you to read it and use it to your advantage when pursuing new business!

June 7th, 2021

To Our Valued Distributors,

As we exit the most severe phases of the COVID-19 Pandemic we have been immediately faced by new and problematic challenges. All sectors of the meat commodities trade, including the poultry, pork and beef markets continue to face intense labor shortages. This has been paired with an increased cost of fuel, transportation and cost of labor. There has also been international frictions that have affected normal international trade that have a disproportional affect on the manufacturing and food industries.

Additionally, this storm of factors has hit as the American public returns to a normalized social setting. This too has created difficulties as many of our fellow citizens look to socialize, celebrate and reunite after more than a year apart. When held against the labor shortages that exist it has been hard for the meat commodity sector to keep up with demands. Many of these companies are reporting the lowest stock of inventory in more then a decade.

With all of these variables to consider the Thumann’s company has been forced to increase pricing across several categories of products. We realize and understand that with an approaching summer that should have strong sales opportunities for you that this does not seem like the opportune time for this adjustment. However, in order to keep our company’s operations viable in the face of the increased costs of raw goods and of our own labor, we must follow the market pricing trends as they rise in order to provide you with the quality product that we bring to market.

Thank you for your understanding,

Thumann’s Management Team

 

 

9/25/20

Thumann Distributors:

All ham prices will increase 20 cents on Monday as the ham supply starts to tighten again. Meat plants in the mid-west have never fully opened as Covid-19 has ravaged the labor force. Now, as earlier in the year, employees are taking paid leave as children remain in the home for remote learning. Boning lines are not able to operate at full capacity and certain cuts of ham, shoulders, and bacon are scarce. Right now, inventories are low but stable. Hopefully, we will not need to resort to further rounds of rationing.

Thank you for your cooperation.

Sincerely,

Thumann’s Management

A Message from Bob Burke Jr.

To all of our valued distributors:
Our competition​ ​has pulled out of most of their chain stores and independent delis ​in the tri-state area and beyond. While we don’t have an official word as to why, the fact is, product has been physically removed from the stores. There is an opportunity for us to fill the need. I urge ​you to stop at competition’s stores and see if Thumann’s can help fill the void for local store owners.

Thumann’s plans to continue manufacturing during these uncertain times. ​ The CDC, USDA, other legitimate Government and media sources are constantly being monitored for the latest information and safety protocols. As of this writing there is some good news. While the science around coronavirus continues to unfold, there is currently no evidence that the disease is transmitted by food – fresh or packaged – according Donald Schaffner, Ph.D., a distinguished professor in the department of food science at Rutgers University.

Thumann’ s has implemented strict safety protocols.

– Plant sanitizing has been increased to multiple times a day

– sanitizing stations have been installed at exit and entry point at the facility – Employee temperature checkpoints are strictly monitored

– Outside personnel have been banned from entering the plant

– sanitizing all door handles and touchables throughout the plant has been increased

– safe distance protocols are in place and strictly enforced

– PPE equipment for personnel is strictly enforced

– Thumann’s is manufacturing daily

We ask your cooperation and assistance in maintaining these protocols for your safety and for the safety of others. As a local manufacturer, Thumann’s understands its responsibility to help feed our community. Safety is our number one concern.

In closing, this is a crisis the likes of which none of us has ever seen or dealt with before. Thumann’s takes its responsibility to you, our employees and our customers very seriously. While anything can happen at a moment’s notice, our goal is to continue manufacturing. We will do our best to update you as the need arises.

Stay safe ! Be well !

Let’s pray this Coronavirus runs its course and we can all get back to life as we knew it.

 

COVID-19 UPDATE

In response to the Coronavirus outbreak, we have installed some new procedures for your safety and ours.  Beginning Monday, March 16 to reduce the amount of outside contact we have installed the following procedures in the shipping offices.

Always call, fax or email your orders ahead of pick up time.   On the South side no one will be permitted inside the shipping office or inside shipping warehouse.  Please call from outside of the building to let us know that you are here for pick up.  Shipping personnel will bring your order outside to you.

On North side, no one will be permitted beyond the freezer vestibule.  Shipping personnel will come bring you your order.  You can also call and let shipping know you are at the loading dock.

Please do your best to order all product at once to avoid add ons at this time. 

Thank you for your cooperation.  

Management
